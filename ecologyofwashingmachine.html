Challenge

What is the Ecology of a Washing Machine?

1. Where it materially comes from and is going to (its lifecycle)

2. Where it developmentally comes from and is going (in terms of prior similar products and future trends in this product area)

3. Other things, infrastructures, and environments with to which it must be connected or collocated to function appropriately

4. Systems of behavior and meaning associated with its function

5. The kind of worlds (ways of living or working) that are enabled and reinforced by this thing and its system

Approach

The thing about this thing called a Washing Machine. The project was an outcome of the Design Seminar class last Fall which aimed at looking at things in our life and the systems its embedded within. Since I came to America, the idea of a lonely divorced Washing Machine far away from all its domestic appliance friends fascinated me.

Learning

Mapping systems of interactions and it's relations to every behavior's. Design is as much about the origin of routines and systems than problem solving by making new 'things'. The project laid the foundation for a new found interest in going to the routes of some of our modern day practices. Its impact on material needs and sustainability.

Advisor
Cameron Tonkinwise

Design Interventions

A systems map encapsulating all the components of the ‘world’ was designed along with a paper proposing three design interventions within the system that could possibly change or alter the way the machine is used. Re-look at possible behavior changes and the interconnected chain reaction that might occur by slightly shifting the way we approach laundry today. The interventions could be futurist, idealistic or even plain fictional. The aim was to be able to envision the systemic affect and effect
of change.

Intervention 1 Division of Labour
Intervention 2 Care for everyone's dirty laundry
Intervention 3 A washing machine that is not ready
