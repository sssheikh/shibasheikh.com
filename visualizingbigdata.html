Challenge

My Music Library – The task at hand was to visually explain the topic assigned in my case, ‘My Music Library’. Having completely understood the context, both its tangible and intangible impressions. The eventual output would be a large printed poster that would clearly convey the content presented through its conscious structure and scale.

Approach

What does music really mean to me? How had my collection grown over the last few years? When did I begin listening to music actively? The kind of technological shifts in listening, collecting and storing music were some of the initial directions of exploration and gathering data.

Collect | Examine | Consolidate

Learning

‘Some distance from your work takes you a long way’. Dan, our instructor, always recommends this perspective after every project. In retrospect the project helped me become comfortable with information that have been too personal and on many occasions even ambiguous. Through critiques and group discussion I got to see and understand data in new light. Keeping certain parameters like heirarchy, clarity and voice always in mind, helped ground my thoughts and find balance in the final output. 

Having spent enough time gathering data, remembering and sifting through gigabytes of music,
I clearly saw a few broad categories emerging that gave further impetus to narrow down the scope of what my visualization will the take the shape of. Some of the categories identified were:

• People
• Artists / Albums
• Places
• Technology
• Share
• Genres
• Cities
• Activities

All of the above categories formed the basic framework that helped me further detail out the information that I thought was either missing or needed some more probing into. For example - I definitely felt that I could spend some time going over the various genres of music and identifying a certain pattern of listening over the last few years.







The visual explorations helped get further clarity on laying out content and its readablity. I decided to work with the concept of a neuron, its multiple connections and see it taking shape to represent my data. The neuron became the centre of the design, in this case the people and gave rise to the various connections between artists and music genres over a period of time. The content structure began to emerge more clearly as the design got further defined.

Structure | Detailing | Iterations | Resolution
