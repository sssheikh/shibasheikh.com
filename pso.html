Challenge

The Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra came to the Service Design class to help them with new directions of their service modal. They gave us an initial briefing of their current model and service flow and the possible areas for interventions. Although, after that we had an ongoing conversation with them throughout the project based on our teams chosen area of work.

Exploratory Research and Findings

Our team explored several possible topic areas. Some of us even attended a few concerts to create a journey map of the symphony going process. What was interesting to find was that there was a clear divide in the actual channels of subscribing to the PSO and the really high quality concert going experience. Neither did it appeal to a younger population of the community. We decided to tackle this problem and look into it further.

Our team was invited to present our findings from the first presentation to the PSO employees. We conducted a workshop with them to better understand their perspective and understanding of their current models of subscriptions and who they thought was benefiting from it. Our new value-flow model shows the shift from a one-way transaction between the PSO staff and supporters, to a two-way exchange of value and support.

We created a low fidelity prototype of an iOS app for the PSO for members and non members to learn more about their events and programs




We presented our concept mockups to the PSO representatives. We created a video sketch depicting the long term investment that a member would make into the community and gradually shift to a subscription model. Added service touch points like postcards after their first visit with a free song download for the true gifting experience.
