Challenge

Making data useful. This was a Microsoft sponsored research project for the Design Graduate Studio course at CMU. My team chose to look at city data and demographics that is often not tapped into when making the big decision of moving to a new place. How can data be aggregated to help people who are choosing a place to live a complete picture of their priorities?

Solution

Our solution to Microsoft was - 'happyhoods'. 'happyhoods' is a service that uses free city data to help people visualize their priorities with respect to place and happiness. A discovery and recommendation service available across platforms. 

Role

I played a key role in planning, design research, facilitating design research workshops, narrative and storytelling, presentations and visual design language.

Design Process

The project went through rigorous stages of the design process. Defining the problem space, exploratory research, generative research, evaluation, followed by refinement and communication.

Our design solution 'happyhoods' was a combination of an interactive website and a mobile app that guided users through the process of finding a place to live. It used the combination of the priorities that users set coupled with city data to find the best match of the neighborhood in a particular city that they chose to move to. The right balance between qualitative and quantitative data is what sets happyhoods aside from all the other realtor services. We even considered a viable business model for happyhoods to collaborate with already existing services like Zillow and Truelia to transition users to the next step of the moving process – finding a home to buy or rent.
