Challenge

The project was a part of my internship experience at Motorola Mobility in the summer of 2013. The brief entailed designing a new generation of wearables that integrate seamlessly into one’s life. 

Solution

The project was an enquiry in the current landscape of fitness and health tracking products about what is really missing in terms of actual behaviour change. ‘able’ was designed as a strategic framework for a future generation of wearable product ecosystem that affords gradual behavioural change towards making healthy choices.

Role

I worked closely with my mentor and manager during the period of internship to work on internal projects as well as work on a personal project of my choice. 'able' was an outcome of the personal project. 

Device ecosystem

The project was an enquiry in the current landscape of fitness and health tracking products about what is really missing in terms of actual behaviour change. Competitive study and conversations with current users of these products brought to light that every single product in the market is targeted towards a motivated and more or less healthy individual – a person already with the drive to keep up with the maintenance and tracking of additional gadgets along with their phones.

The opportunity for design intervention was for people who struggle with motivation and their ability to keep up to stay healthy and make the right choices on a day to day basis.
